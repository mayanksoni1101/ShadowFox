About Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB)

The Royal Challengers Bengaluru (RCB) franchise fields strong teams in both the Indian Premier League (IPL) for men and the Women's Premier League (WPL), reflecting a commitment to talent acquisition and competitive cricket. As of the 2025 season, both teams have made strategic moves in the auction and player retention, aiming for high-impact performances in their respective tournaments.

RCB Men's Team Overview (2025)

RCB’s men’s squad for IPL 2025 embodies a blend of star power, seasoned internationals, and promising Indian talent. The team is captained by Rajat Patidar, while legendary batter Virat Kohli remains a pivotal figure after being retained. The squad has undergone changes, letting go of experienced campaigners like Faf du Plessis and Glenn Maxwell and inducting new faces to fill key roles. The focus has shifted to enhanced bowling and middle-order stability, seen in the acquisition of Josh Hazlewood to lead the pace attack and the addition of Krunal Pandya as a flexible all-rounder.​

    Notable retained players include:

  • Virat Kohli (Batter)
  • Rajat Patidar (Captain, Batter)
  • Phil Salt (Wicketkeeper Batter)
  • Josh Hazlewood (Bowler)
  • Devdutt Padikkal (Batter)
  • Jitesh Sharma (Wicketkeeper)
  • Yash Dayal (Bowler)
  • Krunal Pandya (all-rounder)
  • Bhuvneshwar Kumar (Bowler)
  • Tim David, Rasikh Salam Dar, Suyash Sharma, Romario Shepherd and more.​

RCB’s auction strategy in 2025 focused on strengthening squad depth and flexibility, with several impactful all-rounders and bowlers joining the roster. The rebuilding approach addresses previous gaps, positioning the team as contenders for their long-awaited maiden IPL title.​

RCB Women’s Team Overview (2025)

The Royal Challengers Bengaluru women's team, led by Smriti Mandhana, enters WPL 2025 as one of the top contenders, building on the momentum of successful past campaigns. Key talents have been retained while shrewd signings have boosted squad diversity and experience.​

    Core squad highlights:

  • Smriti Mandhana (Captain, Batter)
  • Ellyse Perry (All-rounder)
  • Georgia Wareham (All-rounder)
  • Richa Ghosh (Batter, Wicketkeeper)
  • Renuka Singh (Bowler)
  • Kate Cross (Bowler)
  • Sophie Molineux (All-rounder)
  • Danni Wyatt-Hodge (Batter)
  • Shreyanka Patil (All-rounder)

  • Asha Sobhana (Bowler)
  • Kanika Ahuja (All-rounder)

The women’s squad features a balance of international stars like Ellyse Perry, Sophie Devine, Danni Wyatt, and emerging Indian talents such as Shreyanka Patil and Renuka Singh. This lineup gives RCB women reliable batting firepower and depth in bowling—a combination that has made them a formidable force in domestic women’s cricket.​

Legacy, Achievements, and Recent Form

RCB men won their maiden IPL title, ending an 18-year drought, largely thanks to stellar performances from Virat Kohli (Orange Cap winner with 741 runs last season) and emerging players like Yash Dayal (highest wicket-taker, 15 wickets). Historically, RCB has endured heartbreaks as runners-up in 2009, 2011, and 2016, but their 2024 turnaround demonstrated resilience and the impact of their refreshed leadership.​
RCB women have made deep runs into WPL playoffs, relying on experienced campaigners while building a core of Indian and overseas talent. Their balanced roster and tactical additions suggest they remain championship contenders in 2025.​

Conclusion

Both RCB men’s and women’s teams showcase ambitious squad construction, retention of world-class players, and auction strategies fine-tuned for competitive edge. With a passionate fan base and improving results, RCB’s 2025 season sets the stage for further accomplishments in the IPL and WPL, reinforcing their status as one of Indian cricket’s premier franchises.

RCB's first IPL Winning

In 2025, RCB won their first-ever Indian Premier League (IPL) title after 18 years since the IPL's inception. They defeated Punjab Kings by 6 runs in the final held at Narendra Modi Stadium, Ahmedabad. This historic win ended their long wait as they had been runners-up three times earlier (2009, 2011, 2016)

Pivotal King Kohli

Virat Kohli was a pivotal figure in the team since its founding in 2008 and contributed significantly to the 2025 IPL win, including top-scoring in the final. He has also won the Orange Cap twice for being the highest run-scorer in IPL seasons (2016, 2024).

RCB's Franchise

RCB is the IPL's all-time leading run-scorer franchise largely due to Virat Kohli and AB de Villiers' prolific partnership and batting records over the years.

Wicket Takers

Other key moments include Yuzvendra Chahal and Shane Watson being among the highest wicket-takers in different IPL years, and Yash Dayal emerging as a leading wicket-taker in the latest seasons.

Men's Team Performances

RCB has qualified for playoffs multiple times and consistently had some of the league's best individual and team performances over its history.

Won Women's Premier League

The RCB women’s team won their maiden Women's Premier League (WPL) title in 2024 by defeating Delhi Capitals in the final with an 8-wicket victory. This was their first-ever trophy in any competition.
