Frequently Asked Questions :

1. Can medicines completely cure tooth pain?

Painkillers and antibiotics only give temporary relief. Lasting relief comes when a dentist treats the real cause like decay, infection, or gum disease.

2. Does teeth scaling damage enamel or create gaps?

No. Scaling safely removes plaque and tartar above and below the gums. It does not damage enamel or create spaces between teeth. Mild sensitivity afterward is normal and usually settles as the gums heal.

3. Can fluorosis stains be removed by scaling?

Scaling removes only surface stains. Fluorosis is an internal stain, so cosmetic treatments like bleaching, veneers, or crowns may be needed.

4. Can all painful teeth be treated with fillings?

Not always. If decay has reached the pulp (nerve), a simple filling will not be enough. Root canal treatment or extraction may be required. Fillings work best when damage is limited to enamel and dentin.

5. Is a cap (crown) needed after root canal treatment (RCT)?

Usually yes. After RCT, the tooth becomes weaker and more likely to fracture. A crown protects the tooth and helps it function well while chewing.

6. Can milk teeth need RCT?

Yes. If a baby tooth has a deep cavity and is expected to stay in the mouth for some time, child-friendly root canal treatment (pulp therapy) can save the tooth and prevent infection or early loss.

7. How can I maintain good oral hygiene?

- Brush twice daily with proper technique and fluoride toothpaste.
- Use a soft or medium toothbrush and change it every 3 months.
- Floss or clean between teeth daily.
- Rinse after meals and visit your dentist regularly.

8. What is the best age to get braces?

Braces are often most effective between 12‚Äì14 years, but the best time depends on jaw and tooth development. Some children benefit from earlier evaluation.

9. Do all wisdom teeth need to be removed?

No. They are removed only if they are impacted, infected, decayed, causing pain, or damaging nearby teeth. Healthy, properly positioned wisdom teeth can be left in place and monitored.

10. Is it important to replace missing teeth after extraction?

Yes. Replacing missing teeth helps maintain proper bite, prevents nearby teeth from drifting, and supports normal chewing and speech.

11. What are the benefits of dental implants?

Implants replace missing teeth with a fixed, natural-looking option. They improve chewing, appearance, comfort, and can help preserve jawbone.

12. Which toothbrush and toothpaste should I use?

Use a soft or medium-bristled brush with a small head and a fluoride toothpaste. Avoid very abrasive pastes and replace your brush every three months or sooner if bristles are frayed.

13. What causes tooth sensitivity?

Sensitivity may be due to worn enamel, gum recession with exposed roots, tooth decay, cracks, or recent dental treatments. A dental examination is needed to find the exact cause.

14. How often should I see a dentist?

Many people do well with a dental visit about every six months, but the ideal interval can range from about three months to two years depending on individual risk, so the timing should be decided with the dentist.
